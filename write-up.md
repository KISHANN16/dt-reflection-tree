# Design Rationale

## 1. Why these questions?

The tree is designed to guide a tired employee through a structured reflection at the end of the day.
The questions are simple but progressively deeper, ensuring that the user does not feel overwhelmed.

Axis 1 focuses on **agency (internal vs external control)**.
Axis 2 focuses on **orientation (contribution vs entitlement)**.
Axis 3 focuses on **radius (self vs broader impact)**.

Each question forces a clear choice without ambiguity, ensuring deterministic paths.

---

## 2. Branching Logic

The tree follows a strict sequence:
Axis 1 → Axis 2 → Axis 3

Each axis builds on the previous one:

* Recognizing control (Axis 1) enables contribution thinking (Axis 2)
* Contribution thinking enables broader impact awareness (Axis 3)

Decision nodes route users based on grouped answers, ensuring consistent outcomes.

---

## 3. Psychological Foundations

* **Locus of Control (Rotter)** → Axis 1
* **Growth Mindset (Dweck)** → reflected in ownership questions
* **Organizational Citizenship Behavior** → Axis 2
* **Self-transcendence (Maslow)** → Axis 3

The tree avoids moral judgment and instead surfaces awareness.

---

## 4. Trade-offs

* Simplicity over depth: fewer but sharper questions
* Fixed options over free text to maintain determinism
* Clear signals over complex scoring systems
